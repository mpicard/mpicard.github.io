### **1.** 

### **Calm / Safety / Healing States**

  

**Neural mode:** Parasympathetic (rest–digest). Reduced cortisol, slower heart rate.

**Levitin cue:** Music around 50–70 BPM or half-time grooves; low rhythmic density.

  

**Production tactics:**

- **Tempo:** 60–70 BPM or half-time at 120–140 BPM.
    
- **Timbre:** Rounded transients, warm saturation, tape or analog drift. Filter resonance below 0.3 Q.
    
- **Harmony:** use stable modal or minor 7/9 chords; long sustains; minimal tension tones (avoid sharp 11ths or major 7ths).
    
- **Progression examples:**
    
    Em9: Cmaj7. Am11.
    
    Dm9: G13.
    
- **Sound design:** Pad fundamentals around 100–400 Hz (the chest range). Add subtle sub (40–60 Hz) sine reinforcement for grounding.
    

---

### **2.** 

### **Focus / Flow / Cognitive Engagement**

  

**Neural mode:** Beta–gamma coupling, dopamine-regulated reward circuits.

**Levitin cue:** Predictable rhythm + subtle novelty = flow state.

  

**Production tactics:**

- **Tempo:** 90–115 BPM for groove, or hypnotic 125 BPM minimal techno.
    
- **Rhythm:** Layer micro-swing or polyrhythmic ghost elements that shift every 8–16 bars.
    
- **Harmony:** use looped modal progressions with one tension tone (♭9 or add9).
    
- **Progression examples:**
    
    Am9: Fmaj7. Gadd9.
    
    Bm7. Dmaj9.
    
- **Sound design:** Bright mids (2–5 kHz) in percs for attentional hooks; evolving LFO on filter cutoff every 32 bars for novelty.
    

---

### **3.** 

### **Catharsis / Emotional Release**

  

**Neural mode:** Dopamine → prolactin rebound (tears, chills).

**Levitin cue:** Sudden harmonic resolution or timbral lift triggers limbic reward.

  

**Production tactics:**

- **Tempo:** flexible; 100–125 BPM works for both ambient and deep techno.
    
- **Harmony:** build suspended or cluster tension, resolve with perfect intervals or drop of harmonic density.
    
- **Progression examples:**
    
    Csus2. Fmaj7.
    
    Gm11: Bbmaj9.
    
    Em9: Dmaj9.
    
- **Sound design:** Use widening (M/S expansion or chorus) at the emotional apex; open highs at release moment (symbolic “light” event).
    

---

### **4.** 

### **Energy / Activation / Movement Therapy**

  

**Neural mode:** Motor cortex coupling, dopamine-driven arousal.

**Levitin cue:** Rhythmic entrainment of gait frequencies (~120 BPM).

  

**Production tactics:**

- **Tempo:** 118–128 BPM.
    
- **Rhythm:** strong kick–offbeat hat drive; sidechain compression to simulate breath.
    
- **Harmony:** minor pentatonic or dorian for grit with uplift.
    
- **Progression examples:**
    
    Em7: Gmaj9.
    
    Fmin9: Abmaj7.
    
- **Sound design:** Emphasize 80–120 Hz kick fundamental (matches walking cadence). Use modulated delay synced to 1/8D or 3/16 for propulsive motion.
    

---

### **5.** 

### **Recovery / Integration / Closure**

  

**Neural mode:** Parasympathetic reset, emotional digestion.

**Levitin cue:** Return to slow tempo, broad spectral warmth, predictable phrasing.

  

**Production tactics:**

- **Tempo:** 60–70 BPM or ambient drift.
    
- **Harmony:** descending fifths, 7→maj7 resolutions.
    
- **Progression examples:**
    
    Dm9: G13. Cmaj7.
    
    Bm11. E9. Amaj7.
    
- **Sound design:** Lowpass everything slowly over last 2 minutes. Add gentle tape hiss or room tone for human closure.
